Why a Norwegian email security provider?

International tools like MXToolbox, EasyDMARC and dmarcian work technically on any domain. They do not, however, offer:

  • Norwegian language for end users — important for non-IT stakeholders (executives, compliance, board members) who need to understand DMARC reports.
  • Data processing inside the EU/EØS with a Norwegian processor agreement (databehandleravtale) — required by Datatilsynet for many use cases.
  • Local DNS provider knowledge — Domeneshop, PrioNet, IDN.no and other Norwegian registrars have distinct interfaces and quirks that international support desks do not handle well.
  • Norwegian procurement compliance — public-sector buyers under Lov om offentlige anskaffelser often require Norwegian-registered suppliers with NUF/AS structure and MVA-registration.
  • Hours-overlap support — incidents at 14:00 CET are handled by people in your timezone, not in California.

The four standards every Norwegian organisation needs

1. SPF (Sender Policy Framework, RFC 7208)

Lists which servers may send email for your domain. Must end with -all (hard fail) for production. Watch the 10-DNS-lookup limit — exceeded by domains using many third-party senders (Mailchimp, HubSpot, Postmark, Salesforce). Most Norwegian Microsoft 365 customers need include:spf.protection.outlook.com.

2. DKIM (DomainKeys Identified Mail, RFC 6376)

Cryptographic signature on each outgoing email. Verifies message integrity. Norwegian Google Workspace tenants commonly use the google selector; Microsoft 365 uses selector1 and selector2. Use 2048-bit keys; rotate annually.

3. DMARC (Domain-based Message Authentication, Reporting and Conformance, RFC 7489)

Tells receiving servers what to do when SPF/DKIM fail. Phases: p=none (monitor) → p=quarantine → p=reject. The full transition typically takes 6–12 weeks for a Norwegian SMB. Configure rua= reporting from day one.

4. MTA-STS + TLS-RPT (RFC 8461 / 8460)

Enforces TLS on incoming SMTP and provides failure reports. Required by NSM Grunnprinsipper but adopted by < 10 % of Norwegian businesses as of mid-2026. Implement after DMARC is at p=reject.

Norwegian regulatory context

  • Datatilsynet (Norwegian Data Protection Authority) — requires technical measures under GDPR Article 32. Lack of DMARC has been cited in breach-notification cases.
  • NSM (Nasjonal sikkerhetsmyndighet) — Grunnprinsipper for IKT-sikkerhet 2.0 recommends SPF, DKIM, DMARC and MTA-STS for all public-sector domains.
  • NorSIS (Norwegian Centre for Information Security) — publishes annual phishing statistics; recommends DMARC for all .no domains.
  • Sektor-tilsyn — Finanstilsynet (finance), Helsetilsynet (health), Statens vegvesen (transport) all reference NSM guidance in their supervisory frameworks.
